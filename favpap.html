There are some papers I found more interesting than others.

Favorite Papers - HPC and DB

  • Kung, Hsiang-Tsung, and John T. Robinson. "On optimistic methods for concurrency control." ACM Transactions on Database Systems (TODS) 6.2 (1981): 213-226.

  • Fan, Bin, David G. Andersen, and Michael Kaminsky. "MemC3: Compact and concurrent memcache with dumber caching and smarter hashing." Proc. 10th USENIX NSDI (2013).
  • Thomson, Alexander, et al. "Calvin: fast distributed transactions for partitioned database systems." Proceedings of the 2012 ACM SIGMOD International Conference on Management of Data. ACM, 2012.
  • Tu, Stephen, et al. "Speedy transactions in multicore in-memory databases." Proceedings of the Twenty-Fourth ACM Symposium on Operating Systems Principles. ACM, 2013.
  • Pelley, Steven, et al. "Storage management in the nvram era." Proceedings of the VLDB Endowment 7.2 (2013).
  • Boyd-Wickizer, Silas, et al. "An analysis of Linux scalability to many cores." (2010).
  • Stonebraker, Michael, and Joey Hellerstein. "What goes around comes around." Readings in Database Systems 4 (2005).

  • Abadi, Daniel, Samuel Madden, and Miguel Ferreira. "Integrating compression and execution in column-oriented database systems." Proceedings of the 2006 ACM SIGMOD international conference on Management of data. ACM, 2006.
  • Bernstein, Philip A., Colin W. Reid, and Sudipto Das. "Hyder-A Transactional Record Manager for Shared Flash." CIDR. Vol. 11. 2011.
